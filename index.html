Seeing is Believing

The Benefits of Picture Books

Do you remember the picture books that first brought you joy? The ones that were read to you before you could read, or the ones that sparked your imagination for the first time? When you think about these stories what do you see? For most of us, we might see our favorite characters or the enchanting setting of our childhood books. We see the illustrations brought to life through our memories. Picture books bring tremendous benefits to kids who are in the early stages of developing their reading skills. Illustrations shown alongside text offer invaluable tools to help kids build understanding, fluency, vocabulary and other foundational literacy skills. The imagery in a picture book brings the pages to life, serving as a visual roadmap for the story. Also children can understand the story easier by looking at the picture.(https://readingpartners.org/blog/picture-books-develop-literacy-skills)(https://readingpartners.org/blog/picture-books-develop-literacy-skills/)
Here are some important benefits picture books bring to children who are learning to read:

  • Introduce children to reading
  • Encourage conversations
  • Create connections
  • Strengthen visual thinking skills
  • Introduce Art
  • Helps children become better listeners
  • Learn new actions
  • Introduce complex concepts in a safe environment
  • Build vocabulary
